01. About

My interests lie broadly in high-fidelity numerical methods and their applications modeling dynamical systems found in natural phenomena and engineering environments. Recent work on problems with efficient hydrodynamic simulation methods in aerodynamics.

I studied computational science and applied mathematics with their surrounding uses in various scientific fields from Georgia Tech and UC Berkeley. Currently Georgia Tech Math & EE and Modeling and Simulation @ Julia Computing

02. Interests

High-Fidelity Numerical Methods

Numerical solutions and continuous simulation of differential equations arising from physical systems

Efficient Parallel Algorithms

Fast iterative methods and preconditioners as applied to large matrix computations using high-performance computing

Adaptive Mesh Refinement

Unstructured and structured methods of increasing resolution at regions of interest for computational saving

Computational Hydrodynamics

Fluid behavior simulation across environments in aerospace engineering, transport, and astrophysics

Network Simulation

Optimizing communication networks with discrete event simulation of distributed edge computing architectures

Analytical Mathematics

Analytical methods for problems and equations arising in physical science and engineering
